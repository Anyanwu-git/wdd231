Ollys Beauty – Individual Project Site Plan

WDD 231: Web Frontend Development · Week 5

Site Plan Overview

Overview

Website Project Topic: Ollys Beauty – Professional Braiding & Beauty Services

This site plan outlines the structure and visual direction for my three–page informational website promoting my braiding business, Ollys Beauty. The website will focus on professional hair-braiding services such as knotless braids, boho braids, feed-ins, passion twists, and natural hair treatments.

The project will help me create a professional online presence for Ollys Beauty while applying the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills I am learning in WDD 231. It is directly connected to my real-life work and will support future marketing, booking, and client communication.

1. Site Name

Site Name: Ollys Beauty – Professional Braiding & Beauty Services

Optional domain ideas: ollysbeauty.co.uk, ollysbeautybraids.com

I chose this name because “Ollys Beauty” is the name I already use for my hair and beauty services. Including “Professional Braiding & Beauty Services” clearly communicates that the site is focused on high–quality braid styles, hair care, and beauty services for clients who want a polished, reliable look.

2. Site Purpose

The purpose of the Ollys Beauty website is to serve as a central hub where current and potential clients can:

  • Browse available braid and hair styles with clear descriptions and photos.
  • View pricing, what is included, and estimated time for each service.
  • Access booking information and policies (e.g., deposit rules, late arrivals).
  • See a gallery of completed hairstyles and before–after transformations.
  • Find care tips and product recommendations to help maintain their braids.

Overall, the site should make it easy for clients to decide which style they want, understand what to expect, and take the next step to book an appointment.

3. Scenarios (User Questions)

These are typical questions that visitors in the target audience might ask. The content on the site will be designed to answer these questions clearly.

  • “What braid styles does Ollys Beauty offer, and how much does each style cost?”
  • “How long will it take to install knotless braids or passion twists, and what hair should I bring?”
  • “Can I see photos of Ollys Beauty’s previous work to help me choose a style?”
  • “How do I book an appointment, pay a deposit, or reschedule my session?”
  • “What products and nightly routines do you recommend to keep my braids neat and lasting longer?”

4. Planned Pages & Content Structure

Home Page

  • Hero section with a strong welcome message and featured braid image.
  • Short introduction to Ollys Beauty and what makes the service unique.
  • Preview cards of popular services (e.g., knotless braids, boho braids).
  • Call-to-action button linking to booking information or contact section.
  • Short testimonial or “Why choose Ollys Beauty?” highlight.

Services Page

  • Detailed list of services: knotless braids, boho braids, feed-in cornrows, passion twists, natural hair treatments, etc.
  • Pricing, what is included (wash, blow-dry, extensions or not), and estimated installation duration for each style.
  • Recommended hair types and quantities to bring for each service.
  • Booking and policy notes (deposits, cancellation, arrival time).

Gallery Page

  • Image gallery of completed hairstyles, organized by style category.
  • Before-and-after photos where available.
  • Short captions describing style name, length, and any special details.
  • Inspiration section highlighting trending styles or seasonal looks.

Note: This HTML document already uses this color scheme for headings, background, borders, and emphasis as requested in the assignment.

6. Typography

The typography for Ollys Beauty balances modern, clean headings with a comfortable body font that is easy to read on both mobile and desktop.

  • Headings: Poppins, sans-serif – used for all <h1>, <h2>, and <h3> tags to give a bold, stylish, and beauty-focused feel.
  • Body Text: Open Sans, sans-serif – used for paragraphs, lists, and smaller sections because it is legible and friendly.
  • Accent / Code: A simple monospace stack is used in this planning document for highlighting hex values and domain suggestions.

This page already imports these fonts from Google Fonts and applies them to headings and body text.

7. Wireframe Sketches (Home Page)

The following descriptions outline the intended layout for the Home page in both mobile and desktop views. Actual wireframe drawings could be sketched on paper or in a design tool, but this text provides a clear structural guide.

Mobile View (Narrow Screen)

  • Top navigation with logo text “Ollys Beauty” and a simple menu icon.
  • Hero section with a full-width braid photo, overlay text “Professional Braiding & Beauty Services,” and a “Book Now” button.
  • Short “About Ollys Beauty” section with a headshot image and 1–2 short paragraphs.
  • Scrolling list of featured services (cards stacked vertically with style name, short description, starting price, and “View Details” link).
  • A small testimonial or “Why Clients Love Us” quote block.
  • Footer with social icons, contact info, and booking reminder.

Desktop / Tablet View (Wide Screen)

  • Horizontal navigation bar with logo on the left and links Home, Services, Gallery, and Book Now on the right.
  • Hero section split horizontally: large braid image on the right and headline + supporting text + “Book Now” button on the left.
  • Three-column feature section showing top services with icons or mini photos and short descriptions.
  • A gallery preview row with 3–4 images linking to the full Gallery page.
  • Wider testimonial band with background accent color and client quotes.
  • Footer with columns for contact details, quick links, and social media.
